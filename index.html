Who am I?

When I first attended College, I was a Technical Systems Management Major in the College of Agriculture, Consumer, and Environmental Sciences. However, I felt that my interests did not quite align, so I decided to transfer into Computer Science after learning Python in the Winter of 2014.

Ever since then, I've loved Computer Science!

Now I am a Computer Science Major in the College of Engineering at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, with a focus in Intelligence and Big Data.

My current interests are software development and Machine Learning. See the featured projects section below!

I am also Course Assistant for CS 196, where each one of us contributes to making this honors course a great experience for freshman looking to persue their interest in Computer Science!

I am always looking for new avenues to explore. Please contact me if you have a inqury about anything!

Pebble-CSGO

My first Pebble application that uses Valve Corporation's Counter Strike: Global Offensive's Game State Integration API. So far it does not display any in-game information, but vibrates when certain elements in the game is activated. This application uses NodeJS for scripting a small backend, Javascript for Pebble's App configuration, and C for Pebble's application.

ClueHunt for HackIllinois

My first iOS application made in Swift for HackIllinois. This is planned to be a side event, where people search the Engineering Quad for QR codes that we set up. I planned out the clue locations and hunt logistics, as well as creating core portions of the iOS application.
